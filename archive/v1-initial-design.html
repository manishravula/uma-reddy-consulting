About

Science that has walked the fields of Telangana

Dr. Ravula Uma Reddy is a senior agricultural scientist serving as Associate Director of Research at the Regional Agricultural Research Station (RARS), Warangal — part of Professor Jayashankar Telangana State Agricultural University (PJTSAU). With a doctorate in agricultural sciences and a career spanning soil science, agronomy, and crop research, he has spent decades translating research-station findings into practical guidance for farmers.

His work covers the crops that define Telangana agriculture: rice–maize cropping systems, cotton (including pink bollworm management strategies featured in regional media), redgram, and soil health management. He has led zonal research and extension advisory programs, seed distribution and training initiatives, and capacity-building for farmers and tribal communities across the Central Telangana Zone.

Through this consulting practice, that experience is now available directly — to individual farmers planning a season, agri-entrepreneurs building a venture, and institutions seeking research and training partnerships.
